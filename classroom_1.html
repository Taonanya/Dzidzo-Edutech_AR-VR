Learning journey

A VR class should feel like a lesson, not just a 3D room.

Each segment has a clear learning job: orient, explore, practice, and review. That structure is what makes the classroom educational instead of decorative.

01

Orientation

Students enter the lab, understand the objective, and review headset safety, controls, and success criteria.

02

Guided exploration

The teacher reveals models, layers, and hotspots so students observe before they manipulate.

03

Active practice

Learners complete tasks in pairs, annotate findings, and submit evidence of understanding.

04

Reflection and review

Progress, teacher notes, and quick feedback are captured so the session leads into the next class.

Educational outcomes

What students should leave with

  • Explain atomic structure using immersive spatial examples instead of flat diagrams alone.
  • Compare elements and compounds by interacting with dynamic 3D models and teacher cues.
  • Complete a practical task, submit a short reflection, and receive review comments.
  • Build confidence with collaborative virtual learning rather than passive watching.
Session brief

Classroom setup

  • Teacher opens the live scene and enables the guided path.
  • Students join through a course link or dashboard shortcut.
  • Attendance is captured at entry and again at checkpoint two.
  • Review work is attached to each submission at the end of class.

Responsibilities

Students, teachers, and admins each have a real job in this classroom.

The experience works when the lesson flow, records, and content operations support each role instead of treating VR as a standalone demo.

Students

Explore and submit evidence

  • Enter the correct lesson from the dashboard.
  • Complete the guided task path in sequence.
  • Upload a response, screenshot, or reflection before exit.
Teachers

Guide, assess, and review work

  • Start sessions, monitor attendance, and release checkpoints.
  • Review submissions and leave structured feedback.
  • Adapt the next lesson using observed performance.
Admins

Keep the ecosystem running

  • Manage courses, libraries, teachers, and timetabled sessions.
  • Track activity, course health, and content readiness.
  • Ensure the class links, media, and records stay active.
